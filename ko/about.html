213F 공시란?

SEC Form 13F is a quarterly report required by the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) from institutional investment managers who exercise discretion over $100 million or more in qualifying assets (primarily US-listed equities).

Key characteristics of 13F filings:

  • •Filed within 45 days after the end of each calendar quarter
  • •Discloses long positions in US-listed equities and equity options
  • •Does not show short positions, non-US stocks, bonds, or most derivatives
  • •Represents a snapshot of holdings at quarter-end — trades within the quarter are not disclosed
  • •NPS's CIK number is 0001608046 on SEC EDGAR

While 13F data has a built-in delay (filed 45 days after quarter-end), it remains one of the most valuable public windows into how institutional investors are positioning their portfolios.

3데이터 출처 & 방법론

US Holdings (SEC 13F)

NPS's US equity holdings are sourced from SEC Form 13F quarterly filings (CIK 0001608046). These filings are required within 45 daysafter each calendar quarter end. They disclose long positions in US-listed equities only — short positions, non-US stocks, bonds, and most derivatives are excluded.

Korean Holdings (data.go.kr)

Korean domestic holdings are sourced from data.go.kr, Korea's open government data portal. NPS discloses its full domestic portfolio annually. Intra-year updates occur when positions cross the 5% ownership threshold (quarterly disclosure requirement under Korean securities law).

Exchange Rate

KRW/USD conversion uses the KRW=X rate from yfinance at the relevant reporting date. All global portfolio values are converted to USD for comparison.

Combined Portfolio Construction

The global portfolio view merges US 13F data with Korean data.go.kr data. US holdings use the latest quarterly 13F snapshot; Korean holdings use the most recent annual disclosure. Both are converted to USD at the reporting-date exchange rate to produce a unified view of NPS's equity portfolio.
